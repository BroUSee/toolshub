How is CGPA converted to Percentage?

Different universities in India use different formulas to convert CGPA to percentage. The most common formula used by universities like AKTU, PTU, and RTU is simply multiplying your CGPA by 10. So a CGPA of 7.5 equals 75%.

Anna University uses the formula: Percentage = CGPA × 9. VTU uses a different formula: Percentage = (CGPA × 7.1) + 11. Always check your university's official formula to get the correct result.

CGPA Grade Scale

In India, a CGPA above 7.5 (75%+) is generally considered First Class with Distinction. Between 6.0–7.5 (60–75%) is First Class. Between 5.0–6.0 (50–60%) is Second Class. Below 5.0 is a Pass class or may require improvement.
